Sacred History

The legendary life and eternal legacy of Veer Tejaji Maharaj

The Divine Journey

Lokdevta Veer Tejaji Maharaj, born in 1074 CE in the village of Kharnal, Nagaur district, Rajasthan, is revered as a folk deity who epitomizes courage, sacrifice, and devotion. His life story is deeply woven into the cultural fabric of Rajasthan, especially among the Jaat community.

Tejaji was the son of Thakur Tahar Singh and demonstrated extraordinary valor from his youth. His legendary act of protecting cows from dacoits and his ultimate sacrifice for dharma made him an immortal figure in Rajasthani folklore. The deity is particularly known for his devotion to protecting cattle and his supernatural powers to cure snake bites.

His marriage to Pemal, daughter of Raimal Parihar of Panner village, is celebrated in folk songs across Rajasthan. The tale of his sacrifice while keeping his promise to retrieve Pemal's cows has made him a symbol of truth and integrity.

Jaat Community Significance

The eternal bond between Veer Tejaji and the Jaat community

Cultural Heritage & Identity

Veer Tejaji Maharaj holds a special place in the hearts of the Jaat community, who consider him their kuldevta (family deity). Born into a Jaat family in Kharnal, his life exemplifies the virtues that the community holds dear: bravery, honesty, protection of the weak, and unwavering commitment to dharma.

The community celebrates Teja Dashmi (Bhado Dashami) with immense devotion, marking the day of his sacrifice. This festival brings together millions of devotees, particularly from the Jaat community, who travel from across India to pay homage at his temples.

Tejaji's legendary acts include protecting cows from dacoits, which resonates deeply with the agricultural community. His supernatural power to cure snake bites has made him a protector deity, and devotees wear amulets with his image for protection.

Folk songs and ballads about Tejaji, known as "Tejaji ke geet," are an integral part of Jaat cultural celebrations, weddings, and festivals. These songs narrate his heroic deeds, his love story with Pemal, and his ultimate sacrifice, keeping his memory alive through generations.

The Jaat community has built numerous temples dedicated to Tejaji across Rajasthan, Haryana, Madhya Pradesh, and Uttar Pradesh, making him one of the most widely worshipped folk deities in North India.

Sacred Temples

Divine abodes across Rajasthan

Parbatsar Temple

The main temple at Parbatsar in Nagaur district is the most prominent pilgrimage site. Built in white marble, this magnificent temple attracts millions of devotees during Teja Dashmi festival. The annual fair is one of the largest in Rajasthan.

Parbatsar, Nagaur District

Kharnal Birthplace

The sacred birthplace of Veer Tejaji in Kharnal village. This temple marks the holy ground where the divine soul took birth. Devotees visit to seek blessings at the origin of his legendary journey.

Kharnal Village, Nagaur

Sursura Samadhi

The sacred samadhi (final resting place) at Sursura where Tejaji attained martyrdom after being bitten by a snake while protecting cows. This is considered the most spiritually significant site for devotees.

Sursura, Ajmer District

Festival & Events

Celebrations that bring devotees together

Bhado Dashami (August-September)

Teja Dashmi - Main Festival

The grand festival celebrating Veer Tejaji's sacrifice, held on the 10th day of the bright half of Bhadrapada month. Millions of devotees gather at Parbatsar for the largest fair. The celebration includes traditional folk performances, religious ceremonies, and cultural programs showcasing Rajasthani heritage.

Year Round

Monthly Pujas & Aartis

Regular worship ceremonies conducted at all major temples. Morning and evening aartis with traditional bhajans and devotional songs. Special prayers are offered on every Dashami (10th day) of each month.

Cultural Events

Folk Music & Dance Programs

Traditional performances of Tejaji ke geet (folk ballads), Kachhi Ghodi dance, and other Rajasthani cultural arts that narrate the heroic tales of Veer Tejaji. These programs keep the oral traditions alive and educate younger generations.

Community Service

Charitable Activities

Following Tejaji's legacy of protecting the helpless, temples organize medical camps, snake bite treatment awareness programs, cattle welfare initiatives, and food distribution (bhandara) for pilgrims and the needy.
